Comparitive Study of Backward Curved Vane Plug Fan for Industrial Use.

Problem Statement: Many industry uses the backward curved fan for various application. Depending upon the number of vanes, speed of rotation and outer diameter, the application area of the fan varied. ANSYS CFX simulations were performed. 2 different outer diameter was studied. Outer diameter was altered to determine the change in outlet pressure. Later, other two parameter were determined which could restore the outlet pressure to original value.
Result: 542mm outer diameter, 7 vanes and 250 rpm created same pressure effect as 270 mm outer diameter, 7 vanes and 1338 rpm, and 270 mm outer diameter, 12 vanes and 250 rpm.
This project was submitted for partial fulfillment of requirement in course of Turbo Machinery.

 
 
Design of Outer Conveyor System to CFC Filler by Merging two Stations at Surya Nepal Factory.

Problem Statement: The existing FOCKE 20SC outer conveyor line was kept dormant as its CFC filler machine was isolated, requiring a separate set of workers to operate on the filler machine. This not only increased labor costs but also increases material handling costs. A convenient solution was provided to merge the existing FOCKE 20SC outer conveyor with the SASIB 20SC outer conveyor in to a single plains CFC filler machine such that the former problems were eradicated.
Mathematical modeling of the problem to identify the shortest and effiecient route, automation, preparation of BoQ and cost estimation, structural design of the conveyor system, SWOT analysis of alternatives, and selection of the best solution was performed.
Result: Proposed design solution of outer conveyor for FOCKE 20SC was a length of 1143±5 cm, before the merge point with the existing SASIB 20SC outer conveyor line. The buffer time for this solution was 3 minutes 40 seconds for FOCKE 20SC machine while 5 minutes 52 seconds for SASIB 20SC. This solution simplified the outer conveying system for FOCKE 20SC, but required some modification to the existing RSFT outer conveyor line.

Design and simulation of Francis Turbine Runner for Betan Karnali Hydroelectric Project for original and reduced head condition.

Problem Statement: Betan Karnali Hydroelectric Project harness 688MW of power from karnali river. Just downstream of the project, there is another expected hydropower construction of Karnali Chisapani Multipurpose Project. It is foreseen that the BKHEP experiences variation of net head from 90.82 m and to 49.16 m due to increment in tail water level after the construction of Karnali Chisapani Multipurpose Project downstream. The hydraulic efficiency of a turbine will definitely not be optimum when the runner designed for initial net head condition is used for reduced head condition. Another important consideration was that the outer diameter should not be allowed change with the change in design variables. If, the outer diameter of the runner changes then the powerhouse spacing (majorly) and the design of auxuliary system changes. This might indicate the possibility for construction of new hydropower, which will be highly expensive.
We decided to evaluate the performance of the runner blade under reduced head condition, and iterate the design to obtain the blade profile that provided highest hydraulic efficiency, with out changing the outer diameter of the runner.
Result:The simulation carried out for net head of 90.82 m at flow rate 92.37 m 3/s and speed 187.5 rpm resulted in hydraulic efficiency of 94.19% at guidevane opening angle of 18.5°. When the same runner is used at reduced net head of 49.16 m with same flow rate 92.37 m 3/s and speed of 187.5 rpm, hydraulic efficiency of 86.33% is obtained at guidevane opening angle of 31°. Further simulations carried out by varying blade profile resulted gain in hydraulic efficiency. The hydraulic efficiency of 90.94 % was achieved for lean angle +5°at guidevane opening of 30°. Thus, the replacement runner with the lean angle +5°having equal diameter as previous can be done with < 5% decrement in efficiency. This allowed replacement of the runner without changing other hydropower components which is beneficial from financial perspective.

 
 
Study of Thin Bio- Film Production for Use as a Transparent, Hydrophobic, Self-Cleaning Cover for Solar Panels.

Problem Statement: One of the most severe factors for the PV degradation and performance reduction is solar soiling. Solar soiling is the deposition of opaque or semi-transparent particle/layer on Solar PV irradiation surface which preclude the overall photon-energy transmission. There are autonomous or semi-autonomous systems available in the market, for periodic cleaning which is energy consuming. Also, with time, there is a record of people abandoning because of difficulty of maintenance.Bio-chemically synthesized hydrophobic thin films can render solutions to solar soiling. These thin films could replace the solar panel glasses in the future with necessary improvements and possible alterations.
Result: Cellulose nanocrystals (CNC) blended with Poly Vinyl Alcohol (PVA) with surface modification by Poly butylene terephthalate (PBT) and powdered silica was used to produce a transparent, hydrophobic film which could act as a self-cleaning cover for soiling prone solar panels. The film exhibited an average transmittance of 92.18% of the incident light in the solar PV working spectra (420nm-700nm). Hydrophobicity was quantified by the water contact angle (WCA) which was an average of 87.25°. The film was durable to photocatalytic and hydrolytic decay but further research is warranted before rendering the film suitable for use as a protective cover for solar panels.

Study of Optimization of Aircraft Separation using the Time Based Separation at TIA, Nepal.

Problem Statement: The Tribhuvan International Airport (TIA) uses the conventional distance-based separation minima for separation of two consecutive aircraft in approach and a basic Time-based separation (TBS) for aircraft in open airspace. This method of time based seperation does not take wind conditions, and its relation to dissipation of wave vortices in to account. Thus, the method does not depend on real time condition to announce seperation to be maintaned betweeen the aircraft.
The possibility of saving time by switching from the Distanced Based Separation (DBS) system to a fully Enhanced Time Based Separation (ETBS) can improve TIA’s approach procedure. We focused on a study of the comparative analysis between the DBS and the ETBS system for TIA, using Monte-Carlo simulation-based upon various parameters that affect separation between the aircraft. The preliminary setup includes random number mapping for weight class of the plane, wind speed, approach speed, and wind direction in MS Excel. An average daily time lag of 79 minutes was observed between DBS and TBS. Hence, implementation of the ETBS system can save TIA almost 20 days of operation annually.
